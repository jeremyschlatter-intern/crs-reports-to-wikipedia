How It Works

Wikipedia is the #1 source for policy information on the internet, but it doesn't incorporate CRS reports as much as it should. Congressional Research Service reports are authoritative, non-partisan analyses produced for Congress — exactly the kind of high-quality source Wikipedia needs.

This tool helps Wikipedia editors by:

  • Finding CRS reports through the official Congress.gov API (13,600+ reports)
  • Automatically matching reports to relevant Wikipedia articles using AI
  • Identifying information gaps — what's in the CRS report but missing from Wikipedia
  • Generating edit suggestions with properly formatted wikitext and {{ '{{cite web}}' }} citations

CRS reports are public domain works of the U.S. government, making them ideal Wikipedia sources. This tool does not make edits to Wikipedia directly — it assists human editors in identifying and formatting improvements.
